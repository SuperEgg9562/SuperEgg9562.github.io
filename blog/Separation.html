Separation

For a very long time, a year to be exact.... I was very uncomfortable with the divorce. I was so upset and angry with all that was going on and being powerless to stop it. eventually I got to the point that I saw suicide as the only option to stop my pain and inner turmoil. I loved my wife and kids so much I could not see a way forward and attempted to take my own life. All it did was confirm some very humbling facts I was too stubborn to see. My wife no longer loved me like I loved her. She blamed me for a lot of things, and took no responsibility for her part in it. And was intent on trying to take a moral high ground to justify her actions there-after. I was naturally confused at first and believed most things she told me. But it soon became clear that both of us were hurting and only one of us was bearing the full weight of the marriage and its destruction. While I struggled with the loss of my kids and my best friend I started to notice some huge flaws in my soon to be ex-wife. Despite this I chose to ignore my senses and fought bitterly to no avail to save my marriage. In the end it was an exercise in self discovery that alerted me to the major flaws my marriage had evolved into. I was essentially trapped in the fact that my best friend was extremely self-serving and cared not for the consequences of her actions where as I had become evidently aware of my own flaws abd their impact on the marriage. It was humbling to say in the least. I was angry at god, at my best friend and at myself. Anyway my inability to give some of the blame to my wife led to all sorts of problems. It made me into a slave to her will and I would often clash with my own personality to save my relationship. This constant wanting to do right at my own expense led to my eventual attempt. I finally had to admit to myself it had become unhealthy for me to maintain contact of any sort with her, and the way forward was distance and complete cutting of ties with her for a time at least until I no longer loved her so deeply. It was scary at first and is still unsettling to know I did all I could to change for someone who really did not love me like I loved her, and made excuse upon excuse for the justification of her actions. I still hurt now and will probably still hurt for a long time. But I am ok and I will be better man for it. She can no longer hurt me as Im fully aware of who I am and Im not my past rather Im going to be what I choose to be as the future is available to those who seek it. It seems I am at a crossroads in life and thats ok. I can get through this as I have wonderfull friends and family who are in my corner and will not abandon me while I walk this path.
