Turning Point

Wednesday 16Th of September

Ive cried a lot these last 10 and a half months, so much so I could fill the pacific ocean ten times over. My marriage is dead in my wifes eyes. I chose to stand while she did not. There is much behind her decision, but all it boils down to is unforgiveness on a Jesus scale. Which is fine in a way. What I dont get is the justification for choosing this way. I will elaborate the things I contributed to its demise in a later post. Either way Ive been on a quest to change, and I asked God to lead the way. Its quite an interesting path and Ive had divine guidance the whole way.

So my wife left one night in early october. It started out with my eldest son being a kid and annoying his mother. I was in the lounge playing a game to wind myself down as I was stressed about the day. My wife was trying to get our youngest to sleep in our marital bed and was feeding him. She got up and disciplined our eldest. He was crying. I stopped what I was doing stormed down to the boys bedroom and told my wife to get out. This did not go down well. I used colorful language but she refused to leave me to sort our son out a different way. I lost my rag and went to pick up our youngest because he had started to cry. Off back to the lounge to try and settle the boy and rescue the night. But my wife followed me and refused to drop it. I had both kids at this point. She attacked me about everything. I lost it now threw my laptop while holding our youngest. I nearly dropped him. But I caught him in time. My wife became hysterical and called the cops claiming I was being violent towards her and that the kids were in danger. She lied right in front of me. I directed my anger away from her and the kids. They were not in danger. I was heart broken, I just wanted her out now. She gathered up her stuff and left witg our boys. It was hard to watch them go. For the next few days I was a complete mess. It eventually led me to question whether my job was worth my marriage. Because the stress of running a farm seemed to be too much. I decided to leave and I was punished for it by a guilt tripping boss who tried to charge me for all kinds of bullshit he just thought up. I left with none of my holiday pay and threats from an asshole who admitted he did that to his last employee. So my good friends took me in and guided me for quite some time. I got better little by little and started talking to friends and then counselors. It was hard to admit the wrongs I had done. Even harder to forgive the wrongs done to me. I struggled particularly with blame I was receiving. I also felt abandoned by her and those who I considered friends. I felt very alone, the loss of my wife and my kids was too much and the voices gained a foothold. I was miserable a lot of the time but it spurred me to seek understanding of why I felt this way. During this time I would pour my eyes over the bible and constantly wait on the lord. He would answer my prayers with scripture and direct me to the appropriate passage. Like I would ask what did he want me to do? And the passage I would get was from the book of Hebrews about his laws to do with marriage, in particular, divorce. Then I would get another one from the book of malachi about how he hates divorce. And to reinforce it He would use more scripture to tell me to not give up. So now I was in a hard place to be, respecting my wifes decision and being obedient to Jesus. So I listened to God, but still accepted her decision. Its in Gods hands now, I just pray for her now. No expectations towards her. But every expectation to my God to deliver.
